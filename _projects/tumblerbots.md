Robotic systems show significant promise for water environmental sensing — water quality monitoring, pollution mapping, and biodiversity data collection. Conventional deployment methods often disrupt **fragile** ecosystems, preventing depiction of the undisturbed environmental condition.

**TumblerBots** proposes a novel framework utilizing a lightweight tumbler system equipped with a sensing unit, deployed via a drone. This design minimizes disruption to the water habitat by maintaining a slow descent. The sensing unit detaches once on the water surface, enabling precise and non-invasive data collection from the **benthic** zone.

The tumbler is *lightweight* and *compact*, enabling drone deployment. The sensing pod — which detaches and descends to the bottom — is equipped with temperature and pressure sensors and a buoyancy system. The buoyancy system is activated upon task completion using a **chemical** reaction that generates 70 kPa of pressure, causing the silicon membrane to expand by 30%, exceeding the 5.7% volume increase required for positive buoyancy.

The tumblers are made from **ecofriendly** materials to minimize environmental impact. They exhibit a low descent rate of 0.8–2.5 m/s, minimizing disturbance to the ecosystem upon water landing. The system demonstrated robustness in moderate to strong wind conditions during outdoor tests.
